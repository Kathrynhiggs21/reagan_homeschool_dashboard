# Tutor Quick Guide — Reagan's Homeschool Dashboard

**Sign in:** **reaganschool.manus.space** — Google sign-in with the account Katy authorized.
**Questions / breakage:** email **spear.cpt@gmail.com**.

## The whole job, in five moves

**1. Open Today.** The day's blocks are listed in order — math, ELA, science, reading, app time, breaks, etc. Glance at the most recent **Note** to see what the last adult flagged.

**2. Work block by block with Reagan.** For each block, do the work together, then mark it **Complete**, **Needs Help**, or **Skipped**. *Needs Help* is the honest answer when something didn't click — use it freely.

**3. Off-script is fine.** If the day pivots (nature walk instead of ELA, kitchen science instead of math, a long read-aloud), **record what actually happened** as a quick note and tag the subjects. AI will help you tag if you ask. The day counts when it's recorded.

**4. Paper and books count.** Reagan can work on a printed worksheet, in a workbook, or in a physical book. Snap a photo with the **camera button**, the photo files into Finished Work and the Drive mirror automatically. Update the **Bookshelf** if she advanced in a book.

**5. Leave a two-sentence handoff Note.** *"Today she nailed multiplication 6-9 but stalled on word problems. Try a visual approach next time."* That single note is what keeps her school continuous.

## You have full authority

A tutor is treated as a trusted adult. You can **edit the agenda** (Agenda Editor or the For Mom & Grandma drawer), **add or swap blocks**, **upload your own worksheets and resources to the Library**, **add an app to the Apps catalogue**, **award bonus Kiwi Coins** when Reagan earns recognition, and **ask Kiwi (or the AI in the editor) for help in plain language** — *"add 15 min of fractions practice," "lighten the afternoon, she's tired," "build a half-day science adventure."* The only adult-locked items are account settings, the four-digit lock code, and family secrets.

## What's automated (you don't have to do it)

Day log, curriculum tagging, weekly analytics, Drive sync (every artifact lands in **Reagan School Hub (Dashboard)** with a clickable link), finished-work archiving, the **nightly agenda email** to Katy (want it yourself? ask Katy to add you), recap emails, tutor handoff PDFs, and forward-plan compounding. **What's *not* recorded: audio, voice, screen, transcript.** The dashboard keeps the roof-level shape of the day — not surveillance.

## If something doesn't work

Refresh → sign out/in → different browser → different device → wait two minutes. If a block won't mark complete, do the work anyway and add a Note. If an app won't open, swap to an equivalent (IXL ↔ Khan, Crash Course Kids ↔ Mystery Doug) or grab a worksheet from the Library and print it. If nothing loads, **run a manual paper session** — open a book, do math from a workbook, talk through a science topic, sketch — and write down what you did. Real school happened. Tell Katy after.

## Reagan, briefly

Tenth-grader's stamina is not the goal — **20-30 minute blocks**, breaks between, snack and movement when she's flagging. She does best when she **sees the plan on the screen** with you. **Kiwi is her companion**, animated and playful by design; she'll never pop up uninvited or ask for the mic. Roblox/Minecraft are reward time, not school time. Her interests (animals, drawing, makeup, crafting) are legitimate paths into real subjects — let her in.
