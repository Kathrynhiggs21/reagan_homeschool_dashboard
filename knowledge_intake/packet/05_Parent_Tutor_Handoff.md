## What Works (Do These)

1. **Start every session with an emotional check-in** (scale of 1-10). If 7+, shorten the block.
2. **Break tasks into tiny pieces.** "Just do this one problem, then we'll decide."
3. **Use graphic organizers for all writing.** Always.
4. **Read math/writing prompts aloud.** Her reading is fine; comprehension spikes when she doesn't have to decode under pressure.
5. **Celebrate effort and growth, not correctness.** "I noticed you kept going even when it was hard."
6. **Let her help someone younger or teach back a concept.** This is a huge confidence builder.
7. **Offer real-world applications and outdoor science.** Crystals, bird watching, cooking.
8. **Use her interests as hooks:** Roblox, preppy fashion, pets (Dutch), shopping, helping kindergarteners.

## What Doesn't Work (Avoid)

1. **Public correction** or drawing attention to her struggles in front of others.
2. **Surprise or pop-quiz style tasks** with no prep.
3. **Large, unbroken writing assignments** on the computer (typing is a specific challenge).
4. **Pressuring her to explain her feelings** when she's already escalated — give space first.
5. **Penalizing missed work** when anxiety was the cause — reschedule without judgment.
6. **Timed tests without extended-time accommodation.**
7. **Saying "You're fine" or "There's nothing to worry about."** Validate first.

## Daily Must-Haves (Accommodations)

- Small-group or 1-on-1 setting for instruction.
- Extended time (up to 50%) on all assessments.
- Multiplication chart, number line, manipulatives always visible.
- Graphic organizers printed and ready.
- Noise-cancelling headphones / fidget / calming corner available.
- Frequent check-ins ("How's your brain doing?").
- Submission via photo or Google Doc to the adult analytics dashboard — **not** Google Classroom.

## Warning Signs (Intervene Early)

- Morning resistance, stomach aches, or "I'm stupid" statements.
- Going quiet, putting head down, or asking for the bathroom repeatedly.
- Tearing up paper, scribbling over her own work.
- Asking for a lot of reassurance ("Is this right? Is this right?").
- Refusing to start a task.

**Response:** Pause. Offer a break, a drink, Dutch cuddles, or 5 minutes of drawing. Then come back smaller.
